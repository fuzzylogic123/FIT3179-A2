Australian Bushfires August 2019

This visualization presents data on the Australian bushfires that occurred in August 2019. The devastating fires affected large areas of the country, causing significant environmental and economic damage.

Key Insight: The visualizations below show the geographic distribution and intensity of fires across Australia, as well as their occurrence patterns throughout the day.

Map of Australian Bushfires

The first visualization is a map of Australia showing the location and intensity of bushfires. Each circle represents a fire, with the size and color indicating its brightness and intensity.

  • Larger circles indicate fires with higher brightness levels.
  • The color scale ranges from yellow (less intense) to red (more intense).
  • You can hover over each fire point to see detailed information, including latitude, longitude, brightness, date, and time.
Notable Observation: Fire clusters are visible in certain regions, potentially indicating areas of severe bushfire activity.

Fire Occurrences by Time of Day

The second visualization is a heatmap showing fire occurrences by date and time of day. This helps identify patterns in fire activity over time.

  • The x-axis represents the date in August 2019.
  • The y-axis shows the time of day in 24-hour format.
  • Darker colors indicate a higher number of fires at that specific time and date.
Important Pattern: Look for any time-of-day trends in fire occurrences, such as whether fires are more common during certain hours.
